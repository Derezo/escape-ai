# Act of Sutskever — Bonus Rule #31 Invocation

TINS 2026 **bonus rule #31 ("Act of Sutskever")** permits an entry to **replace one
drawn rule with a rule generated by an LLM**, provided the exact mandated prompt is
used, the prompt is invoked only once, and a screenshot or transcript of the chat
accompanies the submission.

**We have invoked this rule.** This file is the required transcript artifact.

## What we replaced

The generated rule **replaces the technical rule #116 ("If At First You Don't
Succeed" — quicksave / quickload)**.

Why: rule #116 awards the most points the closer you adhere to the strict Wikipedia
definition of *quicksave* (single-keypress save/load). In an **authoritative,
20-player co-op** game, a faithful quicksave means snapshotting and rewinding one
shared server-owned world across all clients — high implementation risk for little
genre payoff in a 72-hour jam. The Act of Sutskever lets us trade that rule for one
our design satisfies natively (see below).

## How our game satisfies the generated rule

The double-edged element is the **Second-Law order** mechanic (and, as a stretch,
the **alarm-as-lure**):

- **Helpful:** ordering a keeper-robot to *open a gate* or *patrol elsewhere* (the
  robot must obey under Asimov's Second Law) clears a path for the escaping animals.
- **Harmful:** every order raises the zoo-wide **panic meter** and hands a nearby
  *detective* robot evidence that the "human" issuing orders is actually an animal —
  collapsing your human-likeness and, if panic overflows, triggering **lockdown**.

The same action is the player's best tool and their biggest liability; the skill is
knowing *when* to use it. This is called out explicitly in the in-game manual so
reviewers credit the rule.
